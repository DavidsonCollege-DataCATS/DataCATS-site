# About Data CATS

Data CATS (Consulting, Analytics, and Tutoring Services) is a peer-consulting program at Davidson College that connects students, faculty, and staff with trained student consultants for help on data-driven coursework, research, and projects.

## What we help with

Our consultants come from a range of majors and bring a mix of general skill areas — statistics, data visualization, database programming, machine learning — and specific tools like Python, R, SQL, and Power BI. Whether you're debugging a script, designing a study, cleaning a messy dataset, or just not sure where to start, a Data CATS consultant can help.

## How to get help

- **Browse consultants** on the [homepage](/DataCATS-site/) and filter by skill area, tool, major, coursework, or language to find the best match for your project.
- **Drop in** during office hours — no appointment needed. See the homepage for the current location and schedule.
- **Book an appointment** with a consultant who takes them — visit their profile page and click an open slot on their calendar, or check the [team calendar](/DataCATS-site/calendar) to browse everyone's availability at once.

## Who runs the program

Data CATS is directed by [Pete Benbow](/DataCATS-site/faculty/pete-benbow), the James B. Duke Assistant Professor of the Practice in Data Science. Reach out to him with questions about the program, or to inquire about joining as a consultant.
